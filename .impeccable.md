## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are people who want to break away from the default life script and build real sovereignty over time, money, and mind.
They are often in their late 20s to late 30s, commonly in unsatisfying corporate paths or early-stage independent creator/builder transitions.
Their core job-to-be-done is to quickly find a coherent philosophy and practical path for redesigning life through long-term self-investment and structured self-training.

### Brand Personality
Three-word personality: calm, resolute, curious.
Voice should feel humanistic, intellectually rigorous, and emotionally steady.
The interface should communicate quiet strength, depth, and honesty, not authority theater or hype.
Primary first-impression goal: users understand the philosophy within 10 seconds.

### Aesthetic Direction
Direction: dark, editorial, contemplative, Chinese-first reading experience with Deep Teal accents.
Key references: Dan Koe style pacing and structure (clean hierarchy, generous whitespace, philosophy-forward hero, letters-focused flow).
Secondary references: minimalist intellectual personal sites with restrained composition and depth.
Anti-references: hustle-culture pages, SaaS growth-template aesthetics, loud/neon tech-bro styling, crowded blocks, urgency hacks, get-rich-quick tone.

### Design Principles
1. Philosophy first, conversion second: communicate the worldview immediately, then invite subscription.
2. Quiet power over visual noise: restrained composition, strong hierarchy, no hype patterns.
3. Chinese readability as a first-class requirement: high legibility, generous line-height, careful mixed-language typography.
4. Depth over persuasion tricks: avoid manipulative urgency, keep language honest and specific.
5. Calm interaction baseline: respect reduced motion, maintain WCAG 2.1 AA contrast and clear focus states.
